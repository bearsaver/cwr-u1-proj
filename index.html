The Constitutional Convention was an elemental event in the founding of the United States.

While creating a new constitution, the framers laid the foundation for a new government, addressing a variety of issues that had plagued the country since the Revolution.

However, one issue was left comparatively unaddressed: slavery.

This decision is would prove to significantly impact the country and its future.

So how did the Constitutional Convention set the stage for future compromises and conflicts over the issue of slavery?
